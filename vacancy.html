Job Description for Construction Document Controller

A construction document controller's job description includes keeping track of important project documents like project plans, blueprints, vendor contracts and drawings; creating reports for construction managers; keeping documentation safe and backed up; and performing data entry tasks when project documents need updates. Other duties and responsibilities of a document controller include printing out and distributing project documents to managers, vendors and team members; converting paper documents to an electronic format; keeping files organized by project; documenting meetings and uploading documents to the web. This clerical role requires someone with strong computer, communication and organizational skills to work with documents, handle multiple tasks simultaneously and interact with the construction project team.

Education Requirements

Employers usually look for candidates with at least a high school diploma and some experience using software applications like Microsoft Office, construction management applications and document management systems. Some also prefer candidates with an associate or bachelor's degree in business management, engineering or construction management. In addition to having these skills and credentials, construction document controllers usually have work experience handling clerical tasks for a construction company. PayScale.com reported that document controllers received a median wage of $48,470 annually in May 2018, with half making more and half earning less. Pay ranged from below $28,913 for the bottom 10 percent to over $76,763 for the top 10 percent of document controllers. Location and skills also played a factor in earnings. For example, document controllers in Houston earned a slightly lower median income of $47,781, while those who could prepare documents usually earned more.
